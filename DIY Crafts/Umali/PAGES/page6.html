DIY Paper Peony



This craft is definitely perfect for parties, proms, and wedding. It will bring life to the place and you can put them in a table. It easy to make and you won't need to spend a huge amount of money for a peony. You may reuse it because unlike real peony this DIY Paper Peony will not rot. Make any party pleasant with this great DIY!

Materials that you need:


  • Crepe paper in reds and pinks and yellow
  • Heavy floral wire
  • Scissors
  • Ribbon for bouquet
  • Floral tape


Procedure


  1. Cut out an array of petals for your peony (the more petals you cut, the fuller your peony will be!)

  2. Cut a yellow rectangle (about 2”x1”) stretch the crepe paper to its fullest and fringe cut one edge.

  3. Cut a piece of wire to the length that you would like your peony to be.

  4. With a small amount of hot glue attach the yellow paper to the wire and wrap it around to create the stamen.

  5. Stretch the petals previously cut and begin attaching them to the flower with small amounts of hot glue.

  6. Continue to fill your flower with petals until it is nice and full! (As you attach the out petals you can glue them higher up to create a blooming effect.)

  7. Cut a long strip of green crepe paper, stretch it, and attach it to the base of the peony to create a seamless stem.

  8. Cut out 6-9 leaves from the template depending on how many leaves you would like for your peony.

  9. Cut two to three pieces of wire between 4”-6” long.

  10. Gently stretch the leaves, twist the base of the leaf and attach it to the top of the wire using floral tape.

  11. Wrap the floral tape down to the spot you would like to place your next leaf.

  12. Repeat steps 10 and11 until you have the number of leaves you would like.

  13. Using floral tape being wrapping the wire stem of your peony from the base of the flower.

  14. Attach the wire with your leaves on it to the stem of your leaf using the floral tape.

  15. Continue wrapping.

  16. Repeat step 2.

  17. Continue wrapping to the base of the flower.
