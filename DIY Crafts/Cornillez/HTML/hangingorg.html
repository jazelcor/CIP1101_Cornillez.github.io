Hanging Organizer


Installing a hangging board in your room will help you organize and be reminded of your tasks a day, it maybe for schools, work, or events.


Whether you have a board hanging in your home office, your kitchen, your kids’ bedrooms, or anywhere else — it’s pretty obvious that the plain, brown background doesn’t offer much in terms of decorative style. Of course, you’re supposed to cover it up with all sorts of photos, notes, and other interesting things, but that doesn’t necessarily guarantee that it will transform your dull board into something spectacular.

What you need


  • plywood sheet 30x60cm
  • balsa wood (for the pocket)
  • cork sheet
  • A5 plain page notebook
  • wood glue
  • craft knife and ruler


Procedure


  1. Drill 2 small holes near the top of the plywood.
  2. Take an A5 notebook with plain pages and cut off the cover
            using a craft knife -leave the spine intact so the pages
            don't fall out!
  3. Cut out a piece of cork to the same size as your notebook
            (approximately 15 x 21cm)
  4. Lay the notebook and cork onto the plywood and measure
            where you want them to sit
  5. Mark the area with a pencil and cover with PVA glue
  6. Stick down the notebook and cork and leave to dry!
  7. Next is the pocket- take a thin piece of baslsa wood and
            mark out 4 shapes
    • 1 (21x15cm) rectangle
    • 2 (15 x 3cm) rectangles
    • 1 (21x3cm) rectangle
  8. Cut them out with a craft knife and sand down the edges
  9. Leave the pocket to dry before gluing it onto the organiser
  10. Take some leather cord or string and hang
