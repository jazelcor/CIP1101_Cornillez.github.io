Tablet Case


Using an e-reader can save you from schlepping several heavy books along with you when you travel, but if you miss that hardcover feel, we've got a solution for you! This easy-to-make tablet cover upcycles an old book and keeps your device protected, while still looking cool of course. Put your money away and decide to DIY instead. Not only can you customize it to stand out from the crowd, you can also save some serious money by doing it yourself. Here's how to make your own.

What you need


  • An old book
  • Hot glue sticks
  • Felt Paper
  • White Glue
  • Elastic
  • Pair of Scissors


Procedure


  1. Slowly remove the pages of the old book.

  2. Line the Inside Cover.

  3. Glue the felt to the inside of the book.

  4. Make sure to include a little extra felt in the spine of the
           book so that it can close.

  5. Place your e-reader on the inside cover (on the right)
           and measure out where the corners of the device hit.

  6. You want four elastic pieces to stretch over each of
            the corners.

  7. Attach the elastic, use the point of your scissors and poke
            holes where you're like the elastic to go through the cover.

  8. Tie the elastic behind the cover and knot
