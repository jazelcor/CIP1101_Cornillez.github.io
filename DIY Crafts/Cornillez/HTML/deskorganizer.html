Nesting Desk Organizer


Your desk is a gateway to your work state of mind.

It is a colorful explosion of books and decor? A rat's nest of paperwork and coffee cups? Or just a place where notepads go to die? Check out with this DIY that will help you triage, prioritize, and nail down your own little productivity bubble.


One of the most useful accessories when it comes to an organized desk are smart holders that hide everything from pens and pencils to your charging cables, accessories and a whole lot more. A chic, acrylic stand and holder takes both greater effort and time.

What you need


  • Ruler
  • Scalpel
  • Cutting mat
  • Wood glue
  • Sandpaper
  • 1 wooden box or tray
  • 3 panels of basswood
  • Acrylic paint


Procedure


  1. Measure the dimensions of your wooden tray or box and    mark down what sizes you’ll need to build the different       nesting boxes. To build different size boxes, you can replace         our size dimensions with your numbers.
  2. For the first tall box, cut two pieces of basswood with the         X-Acto knife that measure 3" x 4".
  3. To cut the basswood, lightly score the wood a few times and         it will easily cut through.
  4. Cut 2 pieces that measure 2.75" x 4" for the parallel sides.
  5. Cut a 3" x 3 for the bottom of the box .
  6. Paint the inside of box before gluing the wood sides.
  7. Use super glue to glue the sides together in a butt joint.
  8. Allow each side to dry before you continue.
  9. Add glue into the corners to reinforce the hold.
  10. Glue the bottom of the box on.
  11. Sand any rough edges.
  12. Paint the outside of the box once it’s all dry.
  13. Repeat to build the rest of the boxes!
